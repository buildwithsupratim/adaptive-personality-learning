French Revolution Basics

France Before the Revolution

Before the revolution, France was ruled by a king. The king had total power and made all important decisions. Society was divided into different groups, and not everyone was treated equally. Many people lived difficult lives, especially poor farmers and workers.

The Three Estates

French society was divided into three estates. The First Estate included the clergy, such as priests. The Second Estate included nobles, who were wealthy and powerful. The Third Estate included common people like farmers, workers, and merchants. The Third Estate had to pay most of the taxes, even though they were the poorest.

Causes of the Revolution

There were several reasons why people became unhappy. The king spent a lot of money, and France was in debt. Poor harvests caused food shortages, and bread became very expensive. The common people had to pay heavy taxes, while the rich paid little or none. This created anger and frustration.

Storming of the Bastille

On July 14, 1789, people in Paris attacked a prison called the Bastille. They believed it represented the king's unfair power. This event is known as the Storming of the Bastille. It became a symbol of the fight for freedom and equality.

End of Monarchy

As the revolution continued, the power of the king weakened. Eventually, the king was removed from power. France no longer had a monarchy and began moving toward a new form of government.

Results of the Revolution

The French Revolution brought many changes. People gained more rights and equality. The idea that all citizens are equal became important. Although the revolution was a time of struggle, it helped shape modern democratic ideas.
